Ancient Greece (3000 BCE – 146 BCE)

Minoans (2700–1450 BCE): Europe’s first advanced civilization (Crete), known for palaces like Knossos and maritime trade.

Mycenaeans (1600–1100 BCE): Warrior culture of the Trojan War era; inspired Homer’s Iliad and Odyssey.

Archaic Period (800–480 BCE): Rise of city-states (Athens, Sparta), the Olympic Games (776 BCE), and Greek alphabet.

Classical Greece (480–323 BCE):

Golden Age of Athens (Pericles, Parthenon, birth of democracy).

Persian Wars (Battle of Marathon, 490 BCE).

Peloponnesian War (Athens vs. Sparta, 431–404 BCE).

Alexander the Great (356–323 BCE): Conquered from Greece to India, spreading Hellenistic culture.

Roman & Byzantine Eras (146 BCE – 1453 CE)

Roman Greece (146 BCE–330 CE): Became a Roman province but kept Greek culture alive.

Byzantine Empire (330–1453 CE): Christian Greek-speaking empire with Constantinople (Istanbul) as its capital. Preserved ancient texts and built Hagia Sophia.

Ottoman Rule (1453–1821)

Greece fell to the Ottoman Turks after Constantinople’s fall.

Secret schools kept Greek language and Orthodoxy alive.

1821 Greek War of Independence: Revolt led by heroes like Theodoros Kolokotronis, backed by Europe’s philhellenes (e.g., Lord Byron).

Modern Greece (1830–Present)

1830: Recognized as an independent kingdom (Otto of Bavaria as first king).

1923: Population exchange with Turkey after Greco-Turkish War.

WWII: Brutal Nazi occupation (1941–44) and Greek Resistance.

Civil War (1946–49): Communist vs. royalist factions.

1974: Return to democracy after military junta collapses.

1981: Joined the European Union; adopted the euro in 2001.

2004: Athens hosted the Olympic Games (returning to their birthplace).

2010s: Debt crisis and economic recovery.

Greece’s Legacy to the World

Democracy, philosophy (Socrates, Plato), theater (Sophocles), and science (Archimedes).

Olympics, marathon races, and Western art/architecture.

Mythology (Zeus, Hercules) still inspires books and films.
