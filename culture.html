Arts & Expression

Music:

  • Rebetiko (Greek blues) – Born in 1920s tavernas, with themes of love and hardship.
  • Folk dances like sirtaki (from Zorba the Greek) and kalamatianos (circle dances with handkerchiefs).

Theater: Birthplace of drama (tragedies by Sophocles, comedies by Aristophanes).

Iconography: Byzantine-style religious art, seen in churches nationwide.

Religion & Festivals

Greek Orthodox Christianity shapes daily life:

  • Easter: Bigger than Christmas! Midnight Mass, lamb feasts, and candlelit processions.
  • Name Days: Celebrate your saint’s day (often more important than birthdays).

Panigiria: Village festivals honoring saints with music, dancing, and food.

Social Traditions

Philoxenia: Sacred hospitality—strangers are treated like family.

Coffee culture: Frappé or Greek coffee served with loukoumi (Turkish delight), enjoyed for hours.

Plate-smashing (Opa!): Once common at weddings (now mostly tourist shows).

Life Celebrations

Weddings: Crowns (stefana) symbolize unity; guests dance the kalamatianos.

Baptisms: Babies are fully immersed; godparents play a lifelong role.

Funerals: Mourning lasts 40 days; koliva (sweet wheat) honors the deceased.

Culinary Traditions

Meze culture: Small shared plates (olives, feta, dolmades) with ouzo or wine.

Spoon sweets: Preserved fruits offered to guests as a welcome gesture.

New Year’s: Vasilopita cake with a hidden coin for luck.

Folk Customs

Evil Eye (Matiasma): Blue charms ward off envy.

Martis: Red-and-white bracelets worn in March for spring protection.

Karavaki: Decorated boats (not trees!) for Christmas in coastal towns.

Living Antiquity

Language: Modern Greek retains ancient words (e.g., *δημοκρατία/democracy*).

Names: Still use classical names like Alexander, Sophia, Dimitrios.
